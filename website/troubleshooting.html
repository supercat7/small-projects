Troubleshooting Guide

Common Issues & Solutions

No Power
Check PSU connections, power cables, and the power switch on the case. Ensure that the PSU is turned on (some PSUs have a switch on the back).
No Display
Verify that the GPU is properly seated in the PCIe slot. Check that the monitor is powered on and correctly connected to the GPU. If using a motherboard display, make sure the CPU has integrated graphics.
Overheating
Ensure the CPU cooler is correctly installed and thermal paste is applied. Check that the case has good airflow and that all fans are functioning properly.
Strange Noises
Check for loose cables or components that may be vibrating. Inspect the fans and ensure they are not obstructed or damaged.
Blue Screen of Death (BSOD)
This may be caused by incompatible hardware or faulty drivers. Try booting in safe mode, updating your drivers, or running memory diagnostics.
System Freezes
Make sure all components are properly seated. Try updating the BIOS and ensure the RAM is compatible with the motherboard. Check for overheating issues.
No Audio
Ensure that the audio drivers are installed correctly. Check the connections between your audio device and the motherboard or sound card. Verify that the correct output device is selected in your operating system settings.
Slow Performance
Check the task manager for any unnecessary programs running in the background. Ensure you have enough RAM and storage space. Consider upgrading your SSD or adding more RAM if necessary.
